Beat your friends in chess!

I've been playing chess casually and competitively for 2 years, which has given me time to develop a lot of skills, not just in the game itself but in helping others learn how to play better. I've helped many friends improve and am currently trying to help my dad get up to rank 1000 from a complete beginner. I have gotten to rank 1200, which is below professional rank but is above the 90th percentile. I'd be happy to teach anyone of any age, you just have to be willing to learn and adapt.

It's important to start with the basics, and accept that you will make mistakes. Starting from the same basic principals that everyone needs can help you build into a player who can make make lethal tactical moves, or destroy your opponent positionally before you take a single piece.

Starting skills you will learn:

  • Ideal development of pieces
  • How to control the center
  • Importance of castling early
  • Common and uncommon mid-game tactics
  • Checking for checks, captures, and attacks
  • Endgame strategies in many scenarios
